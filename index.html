01 — Who I Am

Me & My Family

My love for Chicano culture started at a young age. I grew up flipping through Lowrider magazines, admiring the artwork, the cars, the bikes, the music — everything that made our culture unique. I spent countless hours drawing little cholo characters, and when the Homies collectibles came out, olvídate — I was hooked.

👤 Photo — roots
02 — My Roots

Culture & Identity

The biggest reason was my father. He never gave up on me. He believed I was capable of more — taught me discipline, respect, and the value of earning an honest living. Today, I'm a husband, a father, and an entrepreneur. But one thing never changed — my love for Chicano culture.

🚗 Photo — brand
03 — The Why

Why Street Scentz

That's why I created Street Scentz. It's a tribute to our raza, our memories, and the culture that shaped us. Each design tells a story through artwork, nostalgia, and culture. The Smell That Hits Home.

1989
Born and raised in New Mexico — surrounded by lowriders, murals, and Chicano culture.
Early 2000s
Running the NM streets — Homies figurines, cholo art, swap meets, Lowrider magazines.
The Turn
Dad never gave up. Discipline and respect pulled me off the streets into the man I became.
2021
Street Scentz is born in Clovis — every scent a reminder of home, la cultura, our raza.
2024
First full collection drops. Six signature scents — from NM car shows to online, the brand goes live.
Today
Husband. Father. Entrepreneur. Still reppin' NM, still cruising. The smell that hits home.
